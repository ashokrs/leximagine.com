Product

What Tickster does

Tickster helps owners scan dogs for ticks using a guided mobile flow designed around close-range coat checks. It supports repeatable scanning habits, calm scan sessions, and better owner preparation before each pass.

Guided close-range scans

Tickster encourages slow close-range passes and practical capture distance instead of rushed full-body sweeps.

Coat and fur preparation

The app reinforces best practices such as good light, gently parted fur, and checking common hiding zones like ears, collar line, belly, armpits, and tail base.

Owner coaching

Tickster helps owners create a steady routine by using a simple scan cue, short first sessions, and reward-based repetition.

Routine guidance

How to prepare your pet, your phone, and the people doing the scan

01

Prepare your pet to scan

Prefer scanning when your dog is sleeping or relaxed. Calm timing makes it easier to part fur gently, reduce motion, and keep the process low stress.

02

Train owners and pets together

Use one simple cue like “Scan,” keep the first checks very short, reward right away, and lengthen the routine slowly as your dog gets more comfortable.

03

Use practical scan behavior

Hold the phone about 3–5 inches from the coat, move slowly, use good light, and check the zones where ticks most often hide.
